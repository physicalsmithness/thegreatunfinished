Why does this happen? (Mechanism)
Step —
  1. The electrons in the double bond reach out and bond with the incoming H⁺.
  2. The H–X bond breaks; X becomes X⁻.
  3. Breaking the π bond leaves a carbocation (C⁺) on one carbon.
  4. Carbocations are more stable when more substituted / resonance-stabilised.
  5. X⁻ attacks the stable C⁺ to complete the product.
