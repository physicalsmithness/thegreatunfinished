Market & timing
Price formation can be myopic (based on belief) or fixed-point (self-consistent adverse selection). Double-auction adds bid/ask dispersion, but quality info is still asymmetric.
Population & quality distributions
Sellers draw quality from a two-component Beta mixture: “lemons” vs “good”. You can tune means and concentration (higher concentration → tighter around the mean).
Costs, valuations & information
Sellers’ reservation price rises with quality; buyers’ willingness-to-pay rises with expected quality. Inspection/information changes selection because informed buyers can reject low quality.
Matching & behaviour toggles
If rematching is enabled, a rejected trade can try another seller (up to a cap). Turn on “rational expectations price” to see classic collapse behaviour more starkly.
Notes: This is an intentionally simplified laboratory. It captures the core feedback loop: low expected quality → low price → high-quality exit → even lower expected quality. Use it as a sandbox, not a calibrated model.
