Space Activity Environment Visualiser

Earth stays fixed on top. The lower panels use your custom gravity and pressure so you can see how three playful activities change in a space habitat: star jumps, a bike launch off a small ramp, and a simplified pillow-fight impact.

What is being simplified

Star jumps: a repeated vertical push from the ground, with body opening and closing in the air. Lower gravity increases airtime and apex height.

Bike ramp: a rider rolls up a small ramp and then follows a drag-and-gravity trajectory before landing. Lower gravity lengthens airtime and landing distance; lower pressure cuts drag.

Pillow fight: one person swings a soft pillow into another. The pillow speed is slowed a bit by drag, while the defender’s post-impact slide depends strongly on floor grip and gravity through friction.

These are deliberately compact, readable models rather than full biomechanics or rigid-body simulations.
