Greenhouse effect: resonance model vs molecular energy levels

A side‑by‑side, conceptual visualisation of how greenhouse gases absorb infrared (IR) and then emit radiation in all directions. Left: a classical driven‑oscillator (“resonance”) picture. Right: a quantised energy‑level picture (rotational/vibrational transitions).

Absorption peaks occur when λ matches an IR-active mode. After absorption, energy can be re‑emitted as IR photons in random directions and/or transferred to thermal motion via collisions.

IB-style link: Both models tell the same story in different language. The resonance model treats the molecule as a dipole oscillator that absorbs strongly near a natural frequency. The energy‑level model treats absorption as a photon promoting a molecule between allowed rotational/vibrational levels. In both cases, the excited molecule can emit in all directions (isotropically) and collisions can redistribute energy into thermal motion.
